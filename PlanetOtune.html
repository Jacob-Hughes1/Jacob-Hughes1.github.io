Planet Otune

Unreal Engine 4 - Group Project

My Role: Designer and Programmer

Overview

Planet Otune is a First-Person Shooter set on alien planet.
You play as a soldier, fighting against the aliens. When a mission goes horribly wrong, you begin to uncover the truth about the aliens and why you're there.
Made as part of a 4 person group as part of my Games Design course at South Staffordshire College. I worked with a 2 artists and an audio designer to create this project.
My work on this project was all of the systems design, level design, in-engine development and mechanic creation utilising Unreal Engine blueprints.

KEY DESIGN FEATURES

Weapon Heat Mechanic

All weapons are laser weapons, there is no ammo count. Instead each weapon uses an overheat mechanic to prevent the player from constantly firing.
The player has access to both an assault rifle and a pistol, each weapon will still continue to cool down while the player is using the other, encouraging regular weapon swaps.

Story Cutscenes

Using the Unreal Engine sequencer tool, three cutscenes were made for this game. These cutscenes were created using timelines, camera rig rails and widget elements for dialogue.
The story is about a team of soldiers being sent to an alien planet to kill them all. After new information comes to light however, the main character must fight for what's right.

Shield Ability

The player is given access to a shield ability which can be activated any time they wish. This ability causes all damage to be mitigated for a short period of time, before going on cooldown for a set amount of time and being unable to be used.
This ability encourages careful timing and positioning, while allowing some forgiveness for the player as they can use it to get out of an otherwise lethal situation.

Enemy Variation

There are three different types of enemies, used to increase challenge and accompany the story of the game.
There are two types of alien enemy: a standard grunt and a brute. These help to make the enemies seem like a big threat during the game.
There is a single type of human enemy that appears in the final section, this enemy is used to show the switching of sides that the main character has gone through.
