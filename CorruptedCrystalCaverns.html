Corrupted Crystal Caverns

Unreal Engine 5 - Solo Project

Overview

Corrupted Crystal Caverns is a top-down shooter and mining game, where the player must fight through a cave of corrupted mining bots while gathering ore and upgrading themselves.
Made as part of my Mechanics Prototyping module during my first year at the University of Staffordshire.
My work on this project was primarily design work, including using Unreal Engine Blueprint systems to create the game mechanics.
The main game loop consists of mining ores around the level using a mining laser, these ores can then be used inside of the shop system to upgrade the player character.

KEY DESIGN FEATURES

Shop System

The player can collect ore from crystals and enemies which can be used to: Upgrade laser, Fix your armour, Buy flares or Buy bombs.
Resources are limited so the player must make informed decisions on what to purchase based on their situation.

Flare Lighting

The level is mostly dark, in order to navigate effectively, the player must use flares to light up their path.
Flares only last for a limited amount of time so the player must think carefully about the correct momements to use them.

Large Crystal Mining

Large Crystals serve a few purposes: Give the player down time between tense moments, showcase the power of the laser, provide resources, communicate to the player where they have yet to explore.

Environmental Hazards

Mining bombs and explosive barrels can be found around the level and are dropped by the bomber enemy. These act as both a hazard to avoid and a tool for the player to use against the enemies within the level.

DESIGN DIAGRAMS

Enemy Miner

The standard enemies, they have the same model as the player as they are from the same mining company, however they have been modified with infectious ore sticking out of their chasis after being stuck in the mines with the infection.

Enemies linger in a zombie-like mindless state, their goal is to protect the ore over anything else.

Once the enemies detect the player, they enter an attack state, keeping close to the player but moving manically around, attempting to destroy the player and eliminate the threat to the ore.

Enemy Bomber

Bombers are the infected defensive systems set up by the mining company, when they spot uninfected mining bots, they enter a panicked defensive state and drop bombs randomly.

When not detecting any uninfected bots, they remain dormant in power saving mode.

Upon detecting an uninfected bot, it moves around randomly and drops bombs in an attempt to stop the player. If the bombs are collided with or shot, they explode dealing significant damage to and surrounding entities.

Player Mining

When the player holds the left mouse button, a laser instantly begins firing out of the tank, this was originally tested with a wind up for a few seconds, but it made the tank controls feel sluggish and clunky especially during combat sections so it was changed to have no wind up.

When destroyed, the ore into multiple chunks which creates a satisfying effect of it breaking into chunks.

The small ore pieces fly towards the player automatically, this prevents the player having to waste time picking everything up off of the floor. It also creates a much more satisfying mining experience.

PROJECT RESTROSPECTIVE

My Reflection after 8 Weeks of Development

ORIGINAL IDEA

The early concepts for this game were mainly inspired by the ideas behind the Outer Wilds DLC: Echoes of the Eye, I loved the way that darkness was utilized in that DLC as a mechanic for the player to work with and fight against, making it the primary obstacle that the player had to learn to overcome throughout the course of the game. Another big inspiration from a game that I loved that used a similar idea is The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom, which uses similar ideas to the Outer wilds DLC in its depths area, but with the player instead needing to use limited resources to light their way through the darkness. I also enjoyed the mechanics behind how the game Deep Rock Galactic allows the player to interact and manipulate light levels, however I didn’t realise at the start of this project just how much inspiration I would take from Deep Rock Galactic.
I took these ideas from games that I absolutely love and decided that I would like to make a game that also features darkness as its primary obstacle. Though it did take a while of idea generation and developing on the idea before I got inspired to set the game in a cave system as a mining robot.

(IMAGE FROM OUTER WILDS: ECHOES OF THE EYE)


IDEA DEVELOPMENT

My first iteration of the idea was going to be set in a city at night, and the player would have to restore power throughout the city which would create a good sense of progression for the player as they restore sections of the city. After developing the idea, I found that I wasn’t particularly interested in the city setting and decided to go back to the drawing board on how to convey my core idea of darkness. It was during Week 5 that my mining idea began to go into its planning phase, as I got a lot more inspired by Deep Rock Galactic, this was also when my idea of corrupted fellow robots would be the enemy of the game, which I have stuck with since.

(IMAGE FROM EARLY DEVELOPMENT PROGRESS OF CITY CONCEPT)


IN-ENGINE GAME DEVELOPMENT

I did run into a few struggles with my Unreal development during this project, I found that because it took me until week 5 to fully come up with my idea that I was settled on, it resulted in me having less time than I would have hoped to develop this in. However, I think with the time that I had, I am quite proud of what I have been able to achieve. My biggest issues that I ran into with this project were trying to make the level design interesting while making it so the player can not get lost too easily due to the darkness, and the struggles with the particle system. I found myself developing a lot of my mechanics before fully starting on the development of my level, due to me putting it off until later as I found myself having more fun with the development of the different mechanics, meaning that by the time it came round to testing, I only had a very short level that took about a minute to complete which sadly meant that I couldn’t gather the most effective of feedback for my testing.
I also struggled with the visual side of the project, particularly with the particle effects that I used for the mining laser, this is still not at the standard that I was hoping for it to be by the end of the project as it does not feel impactful on the ores that the player is supposed to be mining. I do struggle quite a lot with the particle system, and couldn’t find the sufficient online resources in time for me to fix the issue with my laser when it hits the ore, however I feel as though it looks quite nice while it is just shooting and not hitting any ore and I would have loved to be able to make it look as impactful and cool as that while it is actually doing something.
My favourite part of the unreal development was the creation of my shop system and its related mechanics, while it wasn’t completed at the time of testing due to my poor time management, during my testing I asked the question whether my players thought it would be a good inclusion and they all said yes. I think that the reason I wasn’t fully confident on the idea of the shop system myself was because I was still thinking to my planning coming from the rather stripped back experience of Echoes of the Eye and was trying to create a similar feeling still. I am glad that I asked the question to my testers and got the feedback because implementing those mechanics was very fun for me, and it makes the game feel so much better with them now in, as the player has a reason to be mining the things they do as it lets them upgrade their character and get new equipment.

(IMAGE FROM LATE STAGE DEVELOPMENT OF MY LEVEL)


OVERALL:

Overall, I struggled with my time management quite a lot with this project, but I do feel as though despite that I have done quite well for myself. I would like to improve my skills with visuals in the future to help me create more appealing projects, as I believe that they are very important in the overall package of a game, and I would like to be able to extend my skills out to at least be somewhat competent at it.
Something which I also didn’t touch on in this project was any audio, I have done audio before in games and made some of my own music and sound effects before, however this is usually the last thing that I do in my projects to add as finishing touches to the game as I personally do not focus on the audio design of things, despite that I wish that I did get a chance to put some sounds or music, but with my bad time management that was sadly one of the elements that I have had to cut to get this project ready in time.
Sadly due to this bad time management it also impacted my testing results, with me only being able to get 3 people to test my level while it was extremely short, which resulted in feedback that while somewhat helpful, didn't bring anything to my attention that I hadn't noticed myself already through my self testing. This is something that I will make sure to focus on for future projects as my time management is an issue that I have been solving over the course of my time as a student, and I will continue to get better as I go.
I think that with this project I have managed to take my inspiration and make a game that is unique to me with those ideas, I am quite happy with how I used specifically my inspirations from Deep Rock Galactic and The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom to make a cool resource-based adventure through a dark cave system. While I am a little disappointed that I had to tweak my game idea further and further away from the game that I truly wished to create a love letter to, Outer wilds, I think that my skills as a designer are just not quite ready for such a big task as that and the more simple approach that I took while still taking small inspirations has made a product that I am proud of for my current stage in my studies.

Thank you for reading!
