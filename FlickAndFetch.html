Flick & Fetch

Unreal Engine 5 - Group Project

My Role: Design Co Lead and Technical Designer

Winner of the "Best Game Mechanic Award" from the University of Staffordshire!

Overview

Flick and Fetch is an Adventure Archeology Game where players explore procedurally generated caves, uncover fossils, fill their museum!
Made as part of my Junior Collaborative Game Development Module at the University of Staffordshire, Lantern Lit Games was a 27 person team. My role was Technical Designer and Design Co-Lead.
My work on this project was primarily mechanics design, my main goal throughout the project was to design the mechanics and ensure that all members of the tech team were informed on how mechanics should behave.
I also created the tutorial, ensuring to engage the player and onboard them in an effective way.

KEY DESIGN FEATURES

Museum System

The player brings fossils back from their expeditions, which can then be used to unlock museum plaques and showcase the fossil that they have found in their own personal space.

Fossil Gathering Minigame

The player must be careful with their brushing speed and power, if they go too fast then the fossil will break and the minigame will fail.
This ties in directly with the games limited time per run to force the player to prioritise where their time is best used.

Proceduarly Generated Levels

Each level consists of randomly connected level chunks, this ensures that each time the player goes into the cave it feels different.
To ensure that the player doesn't get lost, there are lanterns placed around the level which light up as you go past.

Environmental Hazards

Hazards such as Stalactites, Geysers and Mushrooms with different effects can be found around the level.
Hazards do not hurt the player, instead they all aim to disrupt and slow the player down, eating into their limited time they have in the cave.
